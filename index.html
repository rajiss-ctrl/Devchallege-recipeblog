Classic cheesecake Recipe

☷ Look no further for a creamy and ultra smooth classic cheesecake recipe! Paired
with a buttery graham cracker crust, no one can deny its simple decadence. For
the best results, bake in a water bath.

YEILD 12 Servings
PREP TIME 45 minutes
GOOK TIME 1 hour
TOTAL TIME 7,75 hours

Ingredients

Graham Cracker Crust

1 and 1/2 cups (150g) graham cracker crumbs (about 10 full sheet graham crackers)

5 Tablespoons(70g) unsalted butter, melted

1/4 cup (50g) granulated sugar

cheesecake

four B-ounce blocks (904g) full fat cream cheese,softened to room temprature

1 cup (200g) granulated sugar

1 cup (240g) full-fat sour cream at room temprature

1 teaspoon pure vanilla exract

2 teaspoons fresh lemon juice (optional, but recommended)

3 large eggs at room temprature

topping suggestions: salted caramel, lemon curd,strawberry topping, chocolate ganache, red wine chocolate ganache,fresh fruit,whipped cream, or respberry sauce(recipe in note)

Instructions

Adjust the oven rack to the lower-middle position and preheat oven to 350 ℉(177℃)
Make the crust:Using a food processor, pulse the graham crackers into crumbs.Pour into a medium bowl and stir in sugar and melted butter until combined.(You can also pulse it all together in the food processor.) Mixture will be sandy. Press firmly into the bottomand slightly up the sides of a 9-inche or 10-inch springform pan. No need to grease the pan first. I use a bottom of a measuring cup to pack the crust down tightly. Pre-bake for 8 minutes. Remove from the oven and place the hot pan on a large piece of aluminium foil. The foil will wrap around the pan for the water bath in step 4. Allow crust to slightly cool as you prpare the feelings.
Make the feeling:Using a handheld or stand mixer fitted a paddle attachment, beat the cream cheese and granulated sugar together on medium-highs speed in a large bowl until the mixture is smooth and creamy, about 2 minutes. Add the sour cream, vanilla exract, and lemon juice then beat util fully combined. On medium speed, add the eggs one at a time, beating after reach addition until just blended. After the final egg is incorporated into the batter, stop mixing . To help prevent the cheesecake from deflating and cracking as it cools, aviod over-mixing the batter as best as you can.
Preparing the simple water bath (see note) Boil a pot of water. You need 1 inch of water i your roastin pan for the water bath, so make sure you boil enough. I use entire kettle of hot water. As the water is heating up, wrp the aluminium foil around the springform pan. Pour the cheesecake batter on top of the crust. Use a rubber spatula or spoon to smooth it into an even layer. Place the pan inside of a large roasting pan. Carefully pour the hot water inside of the pan and place in the oven. ( Or you can place the roasting pan in the oven first, then pour the hot water in. Whichever is easier for you. )
Bake cheesecake for 55-70 minutes or until the center is almost set. when it's done, the center of the cheesecake will slightly wobble if you gently shake the pan. Turn the oven off and open the oven door slightly. let the cheesecake sit in the oven in the water bath as it cools down for 1 hour. Removefrom the oven and water bath, then cool cheesecake completely at room temprature. Then refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours or overnight.
Use a knife to loosen the chilled cheesecake from the rim of the springform pan, then remove the rim, using a clean sharp knife, cut into slices for serving. For neat slices, wipe the knife clean and dip into warm water between each slices.
Serve cheesecake with desired toppings. Cover and store leftover cheesecake in the refrigerator for up to 5 days
